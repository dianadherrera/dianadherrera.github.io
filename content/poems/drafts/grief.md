I say:

Memories accumulate,
while decades pass unnoticed.
And the question lingers,
when are you gonna live?

It would be nice.
To forsake the past,
format my hard drive.

Yet repression won't work.
And instead of liberation
it just gets painful.


You say:

Release the emotion 
rooted on the story.

The one that made you throw 
moments into a drawer.

Darkness is scary.
Being confused and unacknowledged 
is a recipe for sorrow.

So open the door
Let them come closer.
Stay calm and sit.
____

Start small and let the door open. 

Let some air enter the room and bring some ease.

Stay outside and sit. 

Let them come close to you, little by little.

Stay calm and still.

Let them sniff you and go around

Meet their eyes with a smile.    / Lower your eyes to meet them / Lower your head to meet their eye.

Let them see the love in your eyes. 

Acknowledge their presence.

Let them lay in your lap or sit around you slowly

Caress them gently and without reservation

Let them feel at ease, 

Be patient until the shaking stops

Let them purr in tranquility

and only then, ask for their story.

They might or might not speak to you,  right away

You might start talking at the same time and laugh a little, at the akwardness

You might stay silent and only feel the others warmth.

You might ask for forgiveness and cry together in relieve

You might let the other know the joy you brought to each other.

Some will stay for a while and some will leave immediatly,

But they will leave on their own. You will not have to do anything.

Offer them their favorite snack and 

give them enough water for their journey home.

Let them know they are always welcome and 

that you would be happy to see them again.

Next time, you just can hang out with some tea and sweets.

Accompany them to the door, wish them safe travels and wave them goodbye.

Iterashai my dear fear! my dear friend!

May you be peaceful and at ease

May you be free
__



Grief

You can live your life in grief

For the last hours you just enjoy

For the last days you were so happy

For the last weeks you were so confident

For the last months you were at peace

For the last years you were delusional

For the last decade that passed you by without noticing it

Your whole life can become grieving

until your last breath

When are you gonna live?

How can I distinguish the line between grieving and life now?

How can I turn off the memories and stop this madness?

How can I be here when all the things around me urge me to go to the past or the future?

Let them go you say? fear arises just with the thought of it

Losing them, forgeting them, forsaken them

No, I wont. I resist to do it



But now again, what does let go means?

I cannot erase my memory, format my hard drive and start all over

My memories will be there as my mind function

But letting go its not meant to erase them or overide them

Make it not look that way and let them not be acknowledge

that is just cruelty, not letting go. thats irresponsible, not letting go

thats pain seeking, not liberation.



Let your memories be, but neutralize the stories in them

You grief because you have identified with the stories behind them, 

You grief because you see the stories behind them as part of yourself, 

Let the memories be facts of what happened, 

causes and conditions that led you here, but nothing more.

Release the emotion that is entangled (enquistado) within them.

The emotion that makes your heart shrink or skip a beat for the mere remembrance

The emotion that makes you put them into a drawer, alone in the dark.

The emotion that makes you cover your ears when you hear them moving.

The emotion that makes you deny them so no one can see or know they are there.

Darkness is scary. They might also be scared, you know? 

Being confused, unacknowledge and repressed is a recipe for pain and sorrow.

They will only howl louder at your fear, at your reprobation, just as a wounded animal.

Feeling ugly enough already. Insecure enough to exist already.
