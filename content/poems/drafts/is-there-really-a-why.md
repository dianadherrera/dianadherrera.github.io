I am a poet
because I said I'd never be
and if I know anything about me,
is that the never's of my life
don't tend to agree.
  
I am a poet, not to craft rhymes
but to call fun into the room.
I heard muses play with you
twice as much if you are an artist,
so I'll humor them through.

I will write them songs
to the sound of the gongs,
describing in metaphors,
the inspiration they brought.

But, now that I think about it
I would rather play them the melody
so, maybe I should be a musician?
Well, performance gives me the creeps
I'll crack in the first intermission.

No, no. A letter should be fine.
I prefer the space words grant, 
they can take it or toss it away
whenever I am not around.

Cause, you know,
I am easily offended
that's why music won't do.
I'll be thinking if they are pretending
if anyone claps to my tune.

Anyway, I'll tell people I'm a poet
then the rumor can spread.
But wait! muses will come
expecting I don't curse, which I do,
thinking I write about love, which I don't.

Maybe I shouldn’t advertise?
I prefer silly and cheeky remarks.
Will they find it improper
if I am just laughing all the time?

Well, well, what it is, is
I may at least write some poems
while I wait for them,
I'll make it my ultimate art,
my Hal to my Lois.

Yet, I feel sorry for my musical talent,
it's just that I can't take my piano anywhere,
it's hard to get good credentials, and
pen and paper, I can fit even in my underwear.

Yes, yes. Poetry is fine.
I can invent my own lingo if I'm ever bored.
*Cocurrum*, anyone?  I'll ask with a straight face.
The shock I'll see in their gaze
will be worthy of shameless zen.

Maybe I'm overthinking it,
Will it be too late to be a painter?
Nah, words were my only companions
through life's crudest conundrums.

I’ll stick to them as I live,
as their crazy disciple.
