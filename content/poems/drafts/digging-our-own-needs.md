Digging our own needs

I always thought this idea proposed by marshal rosenberg was goal oriented. It was strange because I didn't took him for an achiever in that sense. I assumed the idea was to get what was needed. Yet I never expected the courage you needed to ask for it. 

The courage to risk rejection, denial and confront your most insecure parts. To say, it is okay to need. To recognize the need for appreciation, reassurance or validation and ask for it with strength in your voice. To brainstorm for strategies without shrinking, or getting dismissive of the idea by the inevitable contraction in the chest, that tells you it is dangerous to want. It is dangerous to voice to others your deep aspirations, your inner thoughts, they will laugh, they will ridicule you, they will deny you. They know if you can reach them.

The voice that tells you, how stupid you will look if you get dumped mid sentence.

There is bravery in showing yourself
not the easy and flattering parts
but the insecure ones. To say to yourself I need validation and ask for it.
