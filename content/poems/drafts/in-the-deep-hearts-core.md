Walk down Opera street,

cross the dilapidated stone staircase

if you come to the edge

its hundred and two steps will make your head spin


but at its peak the great blue sky

shines brightly, fluffy clouds 

comes as if to hug you

and city lights will be far away.


sit and give your thoughts to the wind as I used to

stay in silence until a far away honk brings you back.

meet with a smile the brave dot 

that becomes a person when it reaches the top


cry and cry into the stony ground

that longs for home


when your mind is empty enough

rise, dust yourself off

bring a prayer to the lips,

feel the wind's kiss.

let the breeze take it with a kiss. (feel the wind's kiss)


turn back, but this time take your left

go around, hike to the church bell

notice the leaves 

their song with the breeze

the sunlight they crave

to transform, to digest.


Descend Michael Angelo's street

take your time, it is steep

now you know peace

you know where I've been.
